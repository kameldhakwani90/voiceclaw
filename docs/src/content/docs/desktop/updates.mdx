---
title: App Updates
description: How VoiceClaw keeps itself up to date and what to do when a new version is ready.
---

VoiceClaw updates itself automatically in the background — you don't need to visit a website or download anything manually.

## What to expect

When a new version is available, VoiceClaw downloads it quietly while you work. Once the download is complete, you'll see two signals:

- **An update banner** appears at the top of the app with the new version number and a **Restart now** button.
- **The tray menu** (top-right of your screen) gains an "Install and Restart: X.Y.Z" item.

You don't have to act immediately. Click **Later** in the banner to dismiss it. The next time you quit VoiceClaw normally, it will automatically start on the new version — no extra step needed.

## Applying an update

You have three ways to apply a staged update:

| Where | What to click |
|---|---|
| In-app banner | **Restart now** |
| Tray menu | **Install and Restart: X.Y.Z** |
| Settings → Updates | **Restart now** |

All three do the same thing: VoiceClaw quits and immediately relaunches on the new version.

## Checking the current version

Open **Settings → Updates**. The card shows:

- The version you're running right now.
- When VoiceClaw last checked for updates.
- A **Check for updates** button if you want to check manually instead of waiting for the automatic 4-hour cycle.
- A **View release notes** link that opens the GitHub release page for your version.

## Update checks

VoiceClaw checks for updates automatically once on launch and then every four hours while it is running. Checks happen silently — you'll only see a notification when an update has actually been staged and is ready to install.

## No internet connection

If your Mac is offline when a check runs, the Settings card will show an error message. No update will be staged, and VoiceClaw will retry at the next scheduled interval or when you click **Check for updates** manually.
